Our Journey

How Usalama Watch Began

Usalama Watch was born in 2021 from a simple yet powerful observation: while technology was advancing rapidly in Kenya, community safety systems remained largely fragmented and inaccessible to ordinary citizens. Our founders witnessed firsthand how lack of timely safety information affected vulnerable communities.

After a series of community meetings across Nairobi, Mombasa, and Kisumu, we identified the critical need for a unified platform that could bridge the gap between citizens, community watch groups, and emergency services. What started as a small pilot project in three Nairobi estates has now grown into a nationwide movement.

Today, Usalama Watch stands as Kenya's leading community safety platform, empowering citizens with real-time safety information and creating stronger, more connected communities.

Our Vision

We envision a Kenya where every community is safe, connected, and empowered; where technology serves as a bridge between citizens and their safety; and where no Kenyan feels alone or vulnerable in their own neighborhood.

By 2025, We Aim To:
  • Reach 1 million Kenyans with safety information
  • Establish watch groups in every urban estate
  • Reduce response time for emergency services
  • Create the largest community safety network in East Africa

Stories That Inspire Us

Kibera Community Transformation

After implementing Usalama Watch in Kibera, break-ins reduced by 65% in the first six months. The community now has an active neighborhood watch with over 200 members.

Kibera, Nairobi
Mombasa Road Safety Success

Real-time accident reporting on Mombasa Road has reduced emergency response time from 25 minutes to under 8 minutes, saving numerous lives through quicker medical attention.

Mombasa Road
Student Safety in Kisumu

University students in Kisumu used our platform to create safe walking routes and reporting systems, resulting in a 80% decrease in incidents around campus areas.

Kisumu
