Safety Tips

Essential Crime Prevention and Personal Safety Advice for Kenyan Communities

Essential Knowledge

Quick Safety Tips

Emergency Numbers

Save emergency contacts: 999, 112, 911. Program them into your phone speed dial and teach children how to use them.

Stay Alert

Always be aware of your surroundings. Avoid using phones while walking in public and trust your instincts about people and places.

Well-Lit Areas

Walk in well-lit areas at night. Park your car in illuminated spots and avoid dark shortcuts through alleys or abandoned areas.

Travel in Groups

There's safety in numbers. When possible, travel with others, especially at night or in unfamiliar neighborhoods.

On The Go

Personal Safety

Walking Safety

Walk confidently and be aware of your surroundings. Avoid displaying expensive phones or jewelry in public.

Public Transport

Use registered matatus and buses. Sit near the driver and keep bags on your lap, not overhead compartments.

Vehicle Safety

Keep doors locked while driving. Park in secure, well-lit areas and check backseat before entering your vehicle.

ATM Safety

Use ATMs in well-lit, secure locations. Shield your PIN and put money away before leaving the machine.

Your Sanctuary

Home Security Tips

01

Doors & Windows

Install quality locks on all doors and windows. Use deadbolts and always lock up when leaving, even for short periods.

  • Install peepholes in main doors
  • Use window security grills
  • Replace flimsy door frames
02

Lighting & Visibility

Keep your property well-lit. Use motion-sensor lights and trim shrubs near windows to eliminate hiding spots.

  • Install outdoor motion lights
  • Use timer switches when away
  • Keep perimeter clear and visible
03

Neighborhood Watch

Join or start a neighborhood watch program. Know your neighbors and look out for each other's properties.

  • Exchange contact with neighbors
  • Report suspicious activity
  • Create community alert groups
04

Security Systems

Consider installing security systems. Even simple measures like alarms or cameras can deter potential burglars.

  • Install alarm systems
  • Use CCTV cameras
  • Get a guard dog if possible
05

When Traveling

Make your home look occupied when away. Use light timers, stop mail delivery, and ask neighbors to watch your house.

  • Use light timer switches
  • Ask neighbors to collect mail
  • Don't announce travel on social media
06

Key Management

Be careful with keys. Don't hide them outside and change locks when you move into a new home or lose keys.

  • Don't label keys with addresses
  • Change locks after moving in
  • Limit who has spare keys

Be Prepared

Emergency Preparedness

Emergency Contacts

Keep a list of emergency numbers by every phone and saved in your mobile. Include police, ambulance, fire, and trusted neighbors.

Police: 999, 112, 911
Ambulance: 120, 112
Fire: 999, 112

First Aid Kit

Maintain a well-stocked first aid kit at home and in your car. Know basic first aid procedures and CPR.

  • Bandages and gauze
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Pain relievers
  • Emergency blanket
  • Flashlight and batteries

Family Emergency Plan

Create and practice a family emergency plan. Include meeting points and communication methods if separated.

  • Designate meeting spots
  • Practice emergency drills
  • Keep important documents safe
  • Prepare emergency supplies

On The Road

Road Safety Tips

Defensive Driving

Always expect the unexpected from other road users. Maintain safe following distances and avoid distractions while driving.

Night Driving

Reduce speed at night and ensure all lights are working. Be extra cautious of pedestrians and cyclists.

Weather Conditions

Adjust driving for rain and poor visibility. Slow down, increase following distance, and use headlights in heavy rain.

Public Transport

Use licensed taxis and matatus. Sit behind the driver when possible and be aware of your belongings at all times.

Cyber Safety Tips

Protect yourself in the digital world with these essential online safety practices

Strong Passwords

Use unique, complex passwords for different accounts and enable two-factor authentication where available.

Secure Connections

Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions. Use VPNs when accessing personal information on public networks.

Privacy Settings

Regularly review and adjust privacy settings on social media. Be cautious about sharing personal information online.

Online Scams

Be wary of unsolicited emails and messages. Never share financial information or send money to unknown parties.
