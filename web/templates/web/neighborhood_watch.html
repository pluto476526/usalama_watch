What is Neighborhood Watch?

Neighborhood Watch is a community-based program where residents work together with law enforcement to prevent crime and build safer neighborhoods. It's about neighbors looking out for each other and creating a united front against criminal activities.

Through Usalama Watch, we make it easy to organize, coordinate, and manage watch groups across Kenya using modern technology while maintaining the core principles of community vigilance.

Get Started

How to Start a Watch Group

1

Gather Interest

Talk to your neighbors and gauge interest. You'll need at least 10-15 households to form an effective watch group.

2

Contact Police

Reach out to your local police station to register your group and schedule initial training sessions.

3

Choose Leaders

Elect a coordinator and block captains who will serve as points of contact and organize activities.

4

Define Area

Clearly define your watch area boundaries and create a map with member locations and contact information.

5

Establish Communication

Set up communication channels - WhatsApp groups, phone trees, or use our Usalama Watch platform.

6

Schedule Meetings

Plan regular meetings to discuss concerns, share updates, and maintain group engagement and training.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any special training to join?

No special training is required to join. Basic orientation and ongoing training are provided by local police and experienced watch coordinators.

How much time do I need to commit?

Time commitment varies by group. Most members spend 2-4 hours per month attending meetings and participating in watch activities.

Are watch groups legal in Kenya?

Yes, Neighborhood Watch groups are legal and encouraged by the Kenya Police Service. They operate as community policing initiatives.

What shouldn't watch groups do?

Watch groups should never confront suspects, carry weapons, or take law enforcement actions. Their role is to observe and report to police.

Can renters join watch groups?

Absolutely! Both homeowners and renters are encouraged to participate. Community safety benefits everyone regardless of ownership status.

How do we communicate effectively?

Most groups use WhatsApp, phone trees, or our Usalama Watch platform for quick alerts and coordination.
